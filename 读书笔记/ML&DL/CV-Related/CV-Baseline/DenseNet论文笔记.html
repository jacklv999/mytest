DenseNet论文笔记

1. Abstract

  • CNN can be deeper, more accurate, and efficient to train if they contain shorter connections
  • they alleviate the vanishing-gradient, strengthen feature propagation, encourage feature reuse, and reduce parameters.

2. Introduction

As CNNs become deeper: information or gradient vanished.

  • ResNets and Highway Networks bypass signal from one layer to the next via identity connections.
  • Stochastic depth shortens ResNets by randomly dropping layers during training
  • FractalNets combine parallel layer sequences with different conv blocks to obtain depth, while maintaining short paths.

They all share a charactrstc: Short Path.

In this paper, we propose an architecture:

  • connect all layers directly with each other.
  • Combine features by concatenate.

NOTE:

we also observe that dense connections have a regularizing effect, which reduces overfitting.

Bottleneck layers

A conv can be as a bottleneck layer before conv to reduce feature-maps, and improve efficiency

Compression

If a dense block contains feature-maps, we let the following transition layer generate output feature-maps

4. Discussion

Model compactness

The concatenation encourages feature reuse, and leads to more compact model.

Achieving the same accuracy, DenseNet-BC requires 1/3 of the params of ResNet.

Implicit Deep Supervision

One explanation for the improvement may be that layers receive additional supervision from previous loss function. Which has shown in deep-suprvsd net.

Stochastic vs. deterministic connection

Although the methods are different, the DenseNet interpretation of stochastic depth may provide insights into the success of this regularizer
