1. Abstract

  • CNN enables networks to construct informative features by fusing both spatial and channel-wise information
  • prior researches seek to strengthen the representational power of a CNN by feature hierarchy
  • we focus on the channel relationship and propose the SE block, that adaptively recalibrates channel-wise feature responses by modelling interdependencies between channels

2. Introduction

Background

CNNs fusing spatial and channel-wise information together within local receptive fields.

CV research is the search for powerful representations that capture the most salient properties for a given task

Innovation

we investigate a different aspect of networkdesign - the relationship between channels. We introduce SE block

it can learn to use global information to selectively emphasise informative features and suppress less useful ones.

3. Related Work

Deeper architectures

improvements to the learning and representational properties of deep networks have:

  • VGGNets showed that increasing the depth could significantly increase the representations
  • BN added stability to the learning process in deep net
  • ResNets show that deeper and stronger nets are feasible in skip connections
  • Highway networks introduced a gating mechanism to regulate the flow of information along shortcut connections.

An alternative, but closely related line of research has focused on methods to improve the functional form of elements:

  • Grouped convs have proven to be a popular approach for increasing the cardinality of learned transformations
  • multi-branch can achieve flexible compositions of operators
Algorithmic Architecture Search

SE blocks can be used as atomic building blocks for these search algorithms, and were demonstrated to be highly effective

Attention and gating mechanisms

SE block comprises a gating mechanism which focuses on enhancing the representational power of the network by modelling channel-wise relationships

5. Model and Complexity

ResNet-50 requires ∼3.86 GFLOPs in a single forward pass, SE-ResNet-50 requires ∼3.87 GFLOPs, corresponding to a 0.26% relative increase over the original ResNet-50.

6. Ablation Study

Reduction ratio

The reduction ratio r allows us to vary the capacity and cost of the SE blocks

r = 16 achieves a good balance between accuracy and complexity

Squeeze Operator

We examine the significance of using global average pooling VS global max pooling

While both are effective, average pooling achieves slightly better result.

Excitation Operator

We next assess the choice of non-linearity for the excitation mechanism.

Different stages

SE blocks bring performance benefits when introduced at each of these stages of the architecture. Moreover, the gains induced by SE blocks at different stages are complementary, in the sense that they can be combined

Integration strategy
